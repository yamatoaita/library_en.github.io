>Proverbs

・A snake swallowing a mosquito
Too small an amount to be of any significance or use.

・A snake knows the snake's path
People of the same kind understand each other's situations, so the actions of one are easily understood by the other.

・A frog mesmerized by a snake
Being so terrified that one is paralyzed and cannot move. Alternatively, being targeted by a formidable enemy with no means of resistance.

・Once bitten by a snake, fears a rotten rope
After a terrible experience, one becomes overly fearful and scared of anything related, even unnecessarily so.

・Half-killing a snake
To torment by leaving something in an unfinished or unresolved state, causing suffering.

・A snake swallows a person even at an inch long
Even when small, a snake has the ferocity to swallow a person. Similarly, a talented person exhibits extraordinary potential even at a young age.

・Missing the long snake
Missing out on a valuable catch or an important opportunity.

・Dragon's head, snake's tail
Starts off strong and impressive but ends in a lackluster or weak manner.
