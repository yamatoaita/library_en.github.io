Folktales about Snakes

・When a snake crosses the path, bad things happen
・If a snake crosses your path, you might drop something, so step back when it happens
・Seeing three snakes means there will be a feast that night
・Seeing a coiled snake brings good fortune
・Seeing snakes mating is auspicious
・If you see snakes mating, it signifies pregnancy
・If you cover mating snakes with an apron, you will receive blessings
・Sleeping with a sash near your pillow causes dreams of snakes
・Dreaming of snakes is a bad omen
・If you dream of snakes, misfortune may occur, and you must pray to your local deity
・Dreaming of a snake entering your bosom means money is coming
・If a snake is buried alive under a house, it’s a bad omen
・Putting a snake’s shed skin in your wallet brings wealth
・"Look at a human's actions, not the snake's feet" (Focus on self-reflection rather than futile inquiries)
・"The crooked nature of a snake" (It is hard to change inherent traits)

Reference: Japanese Folklore Encyclopedia

2. Kumamoto: "Yatsushiro Myoken Festival"

3. Niigata: "The Great Snake Festival"
A festival that uses a giant snake to symbolize lessons learned from floods
